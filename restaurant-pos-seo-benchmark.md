## Verdict Tiers

| Score | Tier | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| 85-100 | RANKING READY | Competitive for long-tail restaurant POS keywords if indexed and technically healthy |
| 70-84 | STRONG PAGE | Good foundation, but needs more proof, depth, or local specificity |
| 50-69 | NEEDS WORK | Can be indexed but unlikely to rank well against serious competitors |
| 30-49 | THIN / WEAK | Too generic, too shallow, or not trust-building enough |
| 0-29 | NOT RANKABLE | Missing search intent, crawlability, useful content, or trust basics |

Do not claim guaranteed ranking. Use this to measure readiness, not to promise outcomes.

## Category 1: Search Intent & Keyword Fit (15 pts)

### 1.1 Primary keyword placement (3 pts)
- 3: Keyword or close variant appears naturally in title, H1, intro, slug, meta title, and one H2.
- 2: Present in most important places but missing one or two.
- 1: Present, but weak or generic.
- 0: Keyword mismatch or missing.

### 1.2 Intent match by page type (3 pts)
- Pillar pages should explain category, use cases, features, buyer criteria, FAQs, and next steps.
- City pages should answer local search intent, not just swap city names.
- Comparison pages should use decision criteria and honest trade-offs.
- Resource pages should be actionable checklists/templates.
- Blog posts should answer a specific operational question.

### 1.3 Long-tail coverage (3 pts)
Page should naturally include variants such as:
- `restaurant POS software India`
- `cafe POS software India`
- `restaurant billing software India`
- `POS software for cafes`
- `restaurant management software India`
- `QR menu for restaurants India`
- `restaurant inventory management software`
- city modifiers like Mumbai, Delhi, Pune

### 1.4 SERP-style answer early (3 pts)
The first 150-250 words should give a clear answer that could stand alone in a snippet or AI answer.

### 1.5 Avoid keyword stuffing (3 pts)
Natural language, no repeated exact-match phrase blocks, no city-name spam.

## Category 2: Industry Depth & Operational Usefulness (20 pts)

### 2.1 Real restaurant workflow coverage (4 pts)
Strong pages should cover order entry, table flow, kitchen status, billing, payments, staff roles, inventory, QR menu, reporting, and owner controls where relevant.

### 2.2 Buyer decision criteria (4 pts)
Include useful criteria: service type, table count, staff roles, menu complexity, offline/online needs, payment flow, inventory maturity, reporting needs, support expectations, and pricing.

### 2.3 Specific operational examples (4 pts)
Use realistic restaurant examples: busy dinner service, takeaway rush, table billing, duplicate payment risk, stockout prevention, QR menu updates, cashier exceptions.

### 2.4 Actionability (4 pts)
Page should help the reader make a decision or complete a task without needing another article immediately.

### 2.5 Content depth for page type (4 pts)
Minimum useful depth:
- Pillar: 1,800-3,500 words
- City page: 900-1,500 words
- Comparison: 1,200-2,200 words
- Resource/checklist: 700-1,500 words plus checklist
- Blog: 900-1,800 words
- Product doc: 500-1,200 words

Thin pages can still be indexed, but should not be considered ranking ready.

## Category 3: Local India Relevance (12 pts)

### 3.1 India-specific buyer context (3 pts)
Mentions India-relevant operations such as UPI, GST/tax settings, QR menu adoption, staff roles, cafe culture, mobile-first usage, and mixed dine-in/takeaway flow where accurate.

### 3.2 City specificity for local pages (3 pts)
City pages must include real local context: service style, target restaurant types, neighborhoods only if true, operational constraints, and buyer intent. Avoid fake local claims.

### 3.3 Language and tone fit (3 pts)
English-first, clear, practical, modern, and operator-focused. Avoid enterprise-heavy jargon and avoid tiny-shop-only billing language.

### 3.4 Local internal linking (3 pts)
City pages link to India pillar, cafe POS, billing software, relevant resources, and comparisons.

---

## Category 4: Trust, Proof & E-E-A-T (15 pts)

### 4.1 Evidence-safe claims (3 pts)
No fake customer logos, fake ratings, fake review schema, fake case studies, or unsupported performance claims.

### 4.2 Author/reviewer signal (3 pts)
Each article/resource should show a named editorial owner or company editorial note, reviewed date, and update date. Human author is ideal once team identity is final.

### 4.3 Real proof when available (3 pts)
Use only real proof: screenshots, customer quotes, onboarding numbers, case studies, support learnings, or product workflow evidence.

### 4.4 Transparent limitations (3 pts)
Pages should honestly say when something depends on restaurant setup, plan, final domain, integrations, or admin activation.

### 4.5 External references where useful (3 pts)
Use credible external references only when they support a real claim: Google Search docs, GST/official sources, payment/provider docs, or industry reports.

---

## Category 5: Conversion & Product Fit (10 pts)

### 5.1 Clear next step (2 pts)
CTA should match intent: create account, view checklist, compare options, read setup guide, or inspect feature page.

### 5.2 Product fit explanation (2 pts)
Explain who the product fits: modern mid-market cafes and restaurants, India-wide, not enterprise-only and not tiny billing-only.

### 5.3 Objection handling (2 pts)
Address common objections: spreadsheets, desktop billing, setup effort, staff training, inventory complexity, QR menu usefulness.

### 5.4 Pricing/plan clarity where relevant (2 pts)
If pricing is mentioned, explain what is included and what is admin-controlled. Avoid hidden or misleading promises.

### 5.5 No aggressive sales copy (2 pts)
Avoid overclaiming, urgency tricks, or generic SaaS language. Operator trust is more important.

---

## Category 6: Technical SEO & Indexability (12 pts)

### 6.1 Indexing controls (2 pts)
`SEO_INDEXING_ENABLED=false` until final HTTPS domain is ready. Robots, canonical, and sitemap behavior must follow config.

### 6.2 Canonical and public URL safety (2 pts)
Canonical URLs must use `PUBLIC_SITE_URL` and never hardcode a temporary domain.

### 6.3 Prerendered/static HTML (2 pts)
Public SEO routes should be prerendered or server-rendered enough for crawlers to see content.

### 6.4 Metadata quality (2 pts)
Unique title, meta description, OG tags, Twitter card, and robots per page.

### 6.5 Structured data (2 pts)
Use appropriate schema: Organization, WebSite, SoftwareApplication, Article/BlogPosting, BreadcrumbList, FAQPage. No fake Review schema.

### 6.6 Sitemap hygiene (2 pts)
Sitemap includes only public indexable SEO routes when indexing is enabled. Private app/auth routes excluded or noindexed.

---

## Category 7: GEO / AI Citation Readiness (10 pts)

### 7.1 Direct quotable answer (2 pts)
Page has a concise, self-contained answer to the main question near the top.

### 7.2 Structured sections (2 pts)
H2/H3 headings are searchable and specific, not generic labels.

### 7.3 FAQ depth (2 pts)
At least 4 strong FAQs per SEO detail page. Answers should be standalone, accurate, and not promotional.

### 7.4 Entity clarity (2 pts)
Clearly states product category, audience, geography, and feature entities: restaurant POS, cafe POS, billing, QR menu, inventory, tables, kitchen, payments.

### 7.5 Comparison tables/checklists (2 pts)
Pages include structured tables, checklists, or decision frameworks that AI systems can summarize.

---

## Category 8: Internal Linking & Topical Authority (6 pts)

### 8.1 Cluster links (2 pts)
Each page links to relevant pillar, city, blog, resource, and comparison pages.

### 8.2 Footer/navigation discovery (2 pts)
Main footer and nav expose SEO sections: Product, Cities, Resources, Compare, Company.

### 8.3 Related content block (2 pts)
Detail pages include relevant next reads, not random links.

## Page-Type Must-Haves

### Pillar Pages
- Clear category definition
- India buyer context
- Feature/use-case sections
- Buying criteria
- FAQ
- Internal links to city, comparison, resources, and blog
- Product CTA

### City Pages
- Real local operational context
- City-specific title/meta/H1
- No fake claims about local customers or offices
- Links to India pillar and relevant resources
- FAQ for city search intent

### Blog Posts
- One specific operational problem
- Direct answer near top
- Practical examples
- 4+ FAQs
- Related pages
- Updated/reviewed date

### Resource Pages
- Checklist or framework
- Scannable sections
- Clear owner/operator action steps
- Download/print can be added later, but page must be useful without it

### Comparison Pages
- Honest comparison table
- Who each option fits
- Trade-offs
- No unfair strawman comparison
- Clear decision recommendation

### Product Docs
- Feature purpose
- Setup steps
- Edge cases
- Role/permission notes
- Related feature links

### Proof / Case Study Pages
- Only real customers or real internal pilot data
- Problem, before state, implementation, result, limitations
- No fake ratings or invented ROI

## Immediate Scoring Rule for Current Restro Content

Current content should not be marked `RANKING READY` unless it has:
- 900+ useful words for blogs or resources
- 4+ FAQs
- direct answer section
- internal related links
- schema support
- no fake proof
- useful operational detail beyond generic POS copy

If a page has strong metadata/schema but thin body copy, cap score at 69.

If a page has fake proof/reviews/logos, cap score at 49 and remove the fake proof before any launch.

If indexing is enabled before final domain readiness, cap technical SEO at 6/12.

By day 60:
- Add real product screenshots
- Add setup docs

- Add real customer proof if available
- Refresh top pages based on Search Console queries
- Add more city/topic pages only after first pages are strong
